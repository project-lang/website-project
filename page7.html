Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

With the increasing use of the Internet for communication, and the increasingly simple ways for information to be disseminated world-wide online with little or no constraints, no matter how private/confidential or explicit, it was becoming apparent there was an abuse of the system whereby personal data was being misused with sometimes devastating consequences for the victims. Additionally it was found that some companies/websites were using private individuals personal information in order to generate revenue for themselves, often without the consumer being aware of this. Step forward General Data Protection Regulation (or GDPR for short). Any country now trading online with a country of the EU must comply with the GDPR. It is now far easier for people to understand how their data is being used, and raise any complaints (which now will have a better chance of being listened to). Sites that have no dealings with EU countries can employ a blocker to block traffic from the EU in order to prevent them falling foul of the GDPR.
